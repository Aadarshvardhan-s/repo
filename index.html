About Genesis

We exist to raise the standard of student innovation — not to chase hype, but to help teams document decisions, test assumptions, and present work the way an institution should.

Genesis Incubation Centre sits inside Genesis Engineering College as a governed pathway from coursework and club activity into serious build cycles. Faculty reviewers, lab coordinators, and external mentors share one rhythm: brief clearly, ship in small steps, measure outcomes.

Whether the project is hardware, software, or a hybrid service, the expectation is the same — evidence over slides, iteration over announcement, and respect for safety, ethics, and IP from day one.

How work moves through GIC

Step 01

Brief & align

Problem statement, constraints, success metrics — signed off before build hours are allocated.

Step 02

Build & test

Small releases, lab validation, and a running log — every change tied to a hypothesis.

Step 03

Review

Studio session with faculty and mentors. Feedback becomes a checklist, not a compliment.

Step 04

Pilot

Partner site or campus trial with tracked outcomes — then the next iteration cycle.
